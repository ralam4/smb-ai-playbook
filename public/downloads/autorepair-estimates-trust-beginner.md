# Write Estimates Customers Actually Understand and Trust
### AI Playbook for Small Business — Auto Repair Guide

Create plain-English estimates, declined repair follow-ups, and vehicle health reports that build customer trust.

---

**Instructions:** Open ChatGPT or Claude. Copy and paste each prompt in order. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own information.

---

## Step 1: List Your Top 10 Most Common Repairs

Gather: repair name, typical parts cost, labor time (hours), total estimate range, and how you currently explain it to the customer. Think: oil changes, brake pads, timing belts, alternators, AC recharge, tire rotations, check engine diagnostics, etc.

---

## Prompt 1: Create Plain-English Estimate Templates

```
I run an auto repair shop and I want to rewrite my most common repair estimates so customers actually understand them and trust my pricing.

Here are my top 10 most common repairs with current pricing:
[PASTE YOUR LIST — e.g.
- Brake pad replacement (front): Parts $45-80, Labor 1.5 hrs, Total $180-280
- Alternator replacement: Parts $200-350, Labor 2 hrs, Total $400-550
- AC recharge and leak check: Parts $30-60, Labor 1 hr, Total $130-200]

For each repair, please rewrite the estimate in customer-friendly language that includes:
1. What's wrong — a plain-English explanation of the problem (no jargon)
2. Why it matters — what happens if they don't fix it (safety, further damage, cost)
3. What we'll do — the actual repair described simply
4. Cost breakdown — parts, labor, and total, with a brief explanation of why each costs what it does
5. How long it takes — so they can plan their day
6. Warranty — what's covered after the repair

Format each as a template my service advisor can fill in and print or email to the customer.
```

---

## Prompt 2: Build a Declined Repair Follow-Up System

```
Many customers decline recommended repairs at my shop. I want a follow-up system that brings them back without being pushy.

My shop details:
- Shop name: [NAME]
- Most commonly declined repairs: [LIST — e.g. 'brake pads when they're at 3mm', 'recommended fluid flushes', 'suspension components', 'timing belt at mileage interval']
- How I currently follow up: [DESCRIBE or 'I don't']

Please create:
1. A same-day follow-up message (text/email) — thank them for their visit, briefly restate what was recommended and why, no pressure
2. A 2-week follow-up — add a bit more urgency with an educational angle ('Here's what can happen if brake pads wear below 2mm...')
3. A 6-week follow-up — a friendly check-in with a soft offer ('We saved your inspection notes — come back this month and we'll waive the re-inspection fee')
4. For each message: keep it under 3 sentences for text, under 5 for email
5. A simple tracking method — how to note which customers declined what, and when each follow-up should be sent
```

---

## Prompt 3: Design a Vehicle Health Report

```
I want to give every customer a simple vehicle health report after their service — something that builds trust and gives them a reason to come back.

My shop name: [NAME]
Services I typically inspect during any visit: [LIST — e.g. brakes, tires, fluids, belts, battery, lights, wipers, suspension]

Please create:
1. A one-page vehicle health report template with sections for:
   - What was done today (the service they paid for)
   - Vehicle vitals check (green/yellow/red status for each inspected system)
   - What to watch for next (upcoming maintenance based on mileage/age)
   - Recommended next visit and what it should include
2. A color-coding system: Green = good, Yellow = monitor/plan for soon, Red = needs attention now
3. A brief explanation script my tech or service advisor can use when handing it to the customer
4. Make it professional enough to build trust but simple enough that a non-car-person understands it immediately
```

---

## What to Expect

**If it works well:** Shops that switch to plain-English estimates typically see declined repair rates drop. The vehicle health report becomes a trust-building tool customers actually keep.

**If it needs tweaking:** Have your front desk person review the AI-generated language — they know which words confuse customers most.

**Long-term value:** The follow-up sequence from Step 3 recovers revenue you're currently leaving on the table. Even a 10% conversion rate on declined repairs adds up fast.
