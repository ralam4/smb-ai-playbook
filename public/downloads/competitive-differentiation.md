# Figure Out What Makes You Different (and Make Sure Customers Know It)
### AI Playbook for Small Business — Universal Guide

Map your competition, find your real differentiators, write your positioning, and embed it across your marketing.

---

**Instructions:** Open ChatGPT or Claude. Copy and paste each prompt in order. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own information.

---

## Step 1: Map Your Competitive Landscape — No Prompt Needed

Before using any AI prompts, identify the following:

- **Your 3-5 closest competitors** — businesses customers would choose instead of you
- **What they charge** for similar services
- **What they emphasize in their marketing** — website, social media, signage
- **What their Google reviews say** — both positive and negative
- **What your own customers have told you** about why they chose you

Write these down. You'll paste them into the prompts below.

---

## Prompt 1: Find Your Actual Differentiators

```
I run a [BUSINESS TYPE] called [BUSINESS NAME] in [CITY].

Here's my competitive landscape:
- Competitor 1: [NAME] — [what they charge, what they emphasize, their review highlights/complaints]
- Competitor 2: [NAME] — [same info]
- Competitor 3: [NAME] — [same info]
(add more if relevant)

About my business:
- What I charge: [PRICING]
- What I think I do well: [LIST]
- What customers have told me they like: [LIST — from reviews, conversations, or feedback]
- What I'd like to be known for: [DESCRIBE]

Please analyze:
1. Compare me vs. each competitor across: service quality, pricing, convenience, specialization, customer experience, brand personality, and online presence
2. Identify my 2-3 strongest differentiators — things I do that competitors can't easily copy
3. Identify any 'table stakes' I'm missing — things competitors offer that I don't, which might be costing me customers
4. Suggest one untapped positioning opportunity — a gap in the market none of my competitors are filling
5. Be honest: if a customer is comparing us side by side, what's the reason they'd choose me? What's the reason they'd choose someone else?
```

---

## Prompt 2: Write Your Positioning Statement

```
Based on the competitive analysis, help me articulate what makes my business different in a way customers immediately understand.

Please create:
1. A positioning statement (1-2 sentences) — the core of what makes me different and who I'm for
2. An elevator pitch (30 seconds / 3-4 sentences) — for networking events, casual conversations, or when someone asks 'what do you do?'
3. A 'Why Us' section for my website (150-200 words) — compelling, specific, not generic
4. 3 talking points my staff can use when customers ask 'why should I choose you?' — natural language, not salesy
5. A tagline or slogan option (5-8 words) that captures my positioning
6. A 'we're NOT for everyone' statement — who is NOT my ideal customer (this actually attracts the right people)
```

---

## Prompt 3: Embed Differentiation in Your Marketing

```
Now help me make sure my positioning actually shows up in my marketing — not just in my head.

My current marketing:
- Website: [URL or describe what's on it]
- Social media: [which platforms, how often I post, what I post about]
- Google Business Profile: [describe current description]
- Other marketing: [signage, flyers, email, referral program, etc.]

Please:
1. Audit my current marketing for generic language — flag any phrases that could describe ANY business in my industry (e.g. 'quality service', 'customer satisfaction', 'years of experience')
2. Rewrite my homepage headline and description around my differentiators
3. Rewrite my Google Business Profile description (750 characters max) with positioning language
4. Create 5 social media posts that reinforce my positioning — each highlighting a different differentiator with a specific story or proof point
5. Suggest one 'signature experience' or touchpoint I could add to my customer journey that reinforces what makes me different
```

---

## What to Expect

**If it works well:** Most owners discover their real differentiator isn't what they expected. The positioning statement gives you — and your staff — a clear answer to "why us?" that isn't "we're the best."

**If it needs tweaking:** If you can't find a differentiator, that's a business strategy problem, not a marketing problem. The exercise itself reveals where you need to invest.

**Long-term value:** The staff talking points from Step 3 have an immediate impact. When every employee can articulate why you're different, every customer interaction reinforces your brand.
