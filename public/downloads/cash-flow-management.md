# Stop Running Out of Cash Before the Month Runs Out
### AI Playbook for Small Business — Universal Guide

Build a cash flow forecast, plan for slow seasons, and create a monthly financial review routine.

---

**Instructions:** Open ChatGPT or Claude. Copy and paste each prompt in order. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own information.

---

## Step 1: Gather Your Financial Snapshot — No Prompt Needed

Before using any AI prompts, collect this information:

- **Monthly revenue for the last 6-12 months** (by month)
- **Fixed monthly expenses** — rent, insurance, loan payments, subscriptions
- **Variable monthly expenses** — supplies, inventory, contractor payments
- **Upcoming known large expenses** — quarterly taxes, insurance renewals, equipment
- **Your current cash reserve** — what's in the bank right now that isn't spoken for

Write these down. You'll paste them into the prompts below.

---

## Prompt 1: Build a 90-Day Cash Flow Forecast

```
I run a [BUSINESS TYPE] and I need to understand my cash flow for the next 90 days.

Here is my financial data:
- Monthly revenue (last 6-12 months): [LIST BY MONTH — e.g. Jan: $18k, Feb: $15k, Mar: $22k...]
- Fixed monthly expenses: $[AMOUNT] (breakdown: rent $X, insurance $X, loan payments $X, subscriptions $X, other $X)
- Variable monthly expenses (average): $[AMOUNT] (breakdown: supplies $X, inventory $X, contractors $X, other $X)
- Upcoming known large expenses: [LIST with dates — e.g. 'quarterly taxes $4,500 due April 15', 'insurance renewal $2,800 due May 1', 'equipment repair $1,200 estimated']
- Current cash reserve: $[AMOUNT]
- Outstanding receivables (money owed to me): $[AMOUNT] expected by [DATES]

Please create:
1. A week-by-week cash flow projection for the next 90 days — showing expected cash in, committed cash out, and net cash position at the end of each week
2. Flag any weeks where my cash position drops below $[MINIMUM COMFORTABLE AMOUNT]
3. Identify my 'danger zone' — the minimum cash I need on hand to cover 2 weeks of operations
4. Suggest 3 specific actions I can take now if any weeks look tight (delay a payment, accelerate a receivable, cut a discretionary expense)
```

---

## Prompt 2: Identify and Plan for Slow Seasons

```
Using my revenue data, help me understand and prepare for my slow periods.

My monthly revenue history:
[PASTE 12+ MONTHS OF REVENUE DATA]

My business type: [TYPE]
My busiest months are typically: [LIST]
My slowest months are typically: [LIST]

Please analyze:
1. Confirm my seasonal pattern — show me the revenue curve across the year and identify my slow season precisely (which months, how much of a dip)
2. Calculate how much cash reserve I need to survive my slowest period without cutting staff or quality
3. Suggest 3-5 strategies to smooth seasonal revenue dips — specific to my business type (e.g. prepaid packages, off-season services, retainer agreements, seasonal promotions)
4. Create a 'slow season prep checklist' — what to do 60, 30, and 7 days before my slow season starts
5. Calculate: if I set aside $X per month during peak season, will I have enough to cover the slow period gap?
```

---

## Prompt 3: Create a Monthly Cash Flow Review Routine

```
I need a simple monthly routine to stay on top of my cash flow — something I can do in 30 minutes.

My comfort level with finances: [e.g. 'I know the basics', 'I have a bookkeeper', 'I just look at my bank balance']
My accounting tool: [e.g. QuickBooks, Wave, spreadsheet, nothing]

Please create:
1. A 30-minute monthly cash flow review checklist — step by step, what to look at and in what order
2. A simple template to compare actual vs. projected revenue and expenses (forecast vs. reality)
3. Three key numbers I should know at all times — and what each one tells me about the health of my business
4. Warning signs that mean I need to take action NOW (not next month)
5. A quarterly 'bigger picture' review — questions to ask myself every 90 days about pricing, expenses, and growth
```

---

## What to Expect

**If it works well:** Most owners are surprised by how predictable their cash flow patterns actually are once they see 6 months of data laid out. The 90-day forecast eliminates most cash surprises.

**If it needs tweaking:** If you don't have clean financial data, start with bank statements. Categorize deposits and withdrawals for the last 3 months — that's enough to spot patterns.

**Long-term value:** The monthly review routine from Step 4 takes 30 minutes and prevents the panicked "can I make payroll?" moments. It's the single most valuable habit a business owner can build.
