# Build a Membership Program That Prints Recurring Revenue
### AI Playbook for Small Business — Car Wash Guide

Design, price, and launch an unlimited wash membership that creates predictable monthly revenue.

---

**Instructions:** Open ChatGPT or Claude. Copy and paste each prompt in order. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own information.

---

## Step 1: Audit Your Current Revenue Mix

Gather: average ticket price by wash type, monthly wash volume, percentage of repeat customers (if known), current pricing for each wash tier, and your monthly fixed costs (rent, utilities, labor, chemicals, equipment).

---

## Prompt 1: Design Your Membership Tiers

```
I run a car wash in [CITY/REGION] and I want to create an unlimited wash membership program.

Here are my current details:
- Wash packages and prices: [LIST — e.g. Basic $8, Deluxe $14, Premium $20, Ultimate $28]
- Average monthly wash volume: [NUMBER] cars
- Estimated percentage of repeat customers (weekly or biweekly): [ESTIMATE]
- Monthly fixed costs (rent, utilities, labor, chemicals): approximately $[AMOUNT]
- My biggest competitor's membership pricing (if known): [DETAILS or 'not sure']

Please help me:
1. Design a 3-tier membership structure (e.g. Basic Unlimited, Plus, Premium) — what's included in each, and recommended monthly pricing
2. For each tier, calculate the breakeven wash frequency — how many times per month would a member need to wash before I start losing money on them
3. Recommend which of my current wash packages to anchor each membership tier to
4. Suggest a sign-up incentive (first month pricing, free upgrade, etc.) that drives trial without destroying margins
5. Compare my proposed pricing to industry benchmarks for my region
```

---

## Prompt 2: Build the Financial Model

```
Using the membership tiers we designed, help me model the financials:
1. If I convert [5% / 10% / 15% / 20%] of my current monthly customers to members, what's my projected monthly recurring revenue for each scenario?
2. Model the 'heavy user' scenario — if the average member washes 8-10 times per month, am I still profitable at each tier?
3. Calculate my monthly breakeven: how many members do I need at each tier to cover my fixed costs with membership revenue alone?
4. Project Year 1 revenue assuming I add [X] new members per month with [Y]% monthly churn
5. What's the worst case — if every member washes every single day, what does that cost me? At what point does that scenario become unsustainable?
```

---

## Prompt 3: Create the Launch Plan

```
I'm ready to launch my car wash membership program. Please create:
1. An email/text announcement to send to my existing customer list — emphasize the value proposition and include a limited-time launch offer
2. Signage copy for 3 locations: entrance sign, pay station, and exit sign — each should highlight a different benefit (savings, convenience, unlimited)
3. A staff script for upselling membership at the register — including how to handle the 'let me think about it' objection
4. A social media launch sequence: 3 posts over 1 week (teaser, launch day, last-chance for launch pricing)
5. A 90-day post-launch review checklist: what metrics to track (sign-ups, churn, average washes per member, revenue per member) and how to know if adjustments are needed
```

---

## What to Expect

**If it works well:** A well-priced membership program can convert 10-15% of regular customers within the first month, creating predictable revenue even on rainy weeks.

**If it needs tweaking:** Start with a simple single-tier unlimited plan if three tiers feels like too much. You can always add tiers later.

**Long-term value:** The financial model from Step 3 is the key decision-making tool — it tells you exactly how many members you need to cover your overhead.
