# Build a 30-Day Content Calendar Without the Burnout
### AI Playbook for Small Business — Universal Guide

Create a content system with pillars, a full month of posts, and a batch production workflow so you never stare at a blank screen again.

---

**Instructions:** Open ChatGPT or Claude. Copy and paste each prompt in order. Fill in the bracketed sections with your own information.

---

## Step 1: Define Your Content Foundations — No Prompt Needed

Before using any AI prompts, gather this information:

- **Your business type** (e.g. bakery, plumbing company, fitness studio)
- **Who your ideal customer is** — age, interests, what they care about
- **Which platforms you're active on** (or want to be)
- **How often you currently post**
- **3-5 topics you could talk about authentically** — your expertise, behind-the-scenes, customer stories, industry tips

Write these down. You'll paste them into the prompts below.

---

## Prompt 1: Generate Your Content Pillars and Post Types

```
I run a [BUSINESS TYPE] in [CITY].

My ideal customer is: [DESCRIBE — e.g. 'busy parents aged 30-45 who want convenience', 'homeowners looking for reliable service', 'young professionals who care about quality']

I'm active on: [PLATFORMS — e.g. Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, LinkedIn]

Topics I can speak to authentically:
- [TOPIC 1 — e.g. 'behind-the-scenes of how we work']
- [TOPIC 2 — e.g. 'tips and education for customers']
- [TOPIC 3 — e.g. 'customer transformations or success stories']
- [TOPIC 4 — e.g. 'local community involvement']
- [TOPIC 5 — e.g. 'my journey as a business owner']

Please create:
1. 4-5 content pillars for my business — each with a name, description, and why it resonates with my audience
2. For each pillar, suggest 3 specific post types (e.g. educational tip, behind-the-scenes video, customer spotlight, before/after, poll/question)
3. A recommended weekly posting rhythm — how many times per week to post on each platform, and which pillars to rotate through
4. A list of 10 'evergreen' post ideas I can recycle every few months
```

---

## Prompt 2: Build the 30-Day Calendar

```
Using the content pillars we created, please generate a full 30-day content calendar.

For each day, include:
1. The content pillar it falls under
2. Post topic/idea (specific, not vague)
3. A draft caption (ready to edit and post)
4. Suggested visual format: photo, carousel, short video/reel, story, or text post
5. Best time to post based on general engagement data for [MY PLATFORMS]
6. Any relevant hashtags (5-10 per post, mix of broad and niche)

Organize by week (Week 1-4) and include a mix of pillars each week.
For promotional posts, keep them to no more than 20% of total content.
```

---

## Prompt 3: Create a Batch Production Workflow

```
I want to produce all my content in one 2-3 hour session per month instead of scrambling every day.

My situation:
- I [do/don't] have someone to help with content
- I'm comfortable with: [photo / video / both / neither — I need to start simple]
- Tools I currently use or am willing to try: [e.g. 'Canva, my phone camera, CapCut' or 'nothing yet']

Please create:
1. A step-by-step monthly batching workflow:
   - How to plan the shoot/content gathering (30 min)
   - How to capture all photos and videos in one session (60 min)
   - How to write all captions in one sitting (45 min)
   - How to schedule everything using a free tool (30 min)
2. A shot list template I can use each month — based on my content pillars, what specific photos/videos do I need?
3. A recommended scheduling tool (free) and how to set it up
4. A monthly content batching checklist I can print and follow every month
```

---

## What to Expect

**If it works well:** Having 30 days of content pre-planned eliminates the "what should I post?" paralysis. Most owners find they can batch-produce a month of content in 2-3 hours.

**If it needs tweaking:** Don't try to be on every platform. Pick 1-2 where your customers actually are and focus there.

**Long-term value:** The batch production workflow from Step 4 is what makes this sustainable. Without a system, even great content plans die after week 2.
